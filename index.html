About Latin

Latin originated in the region of Latium, in central Italy, around the 1st millennium BCE. It was initially the language of the small city-state of Rome and its surrounding tribes. As Rome expanded through conquest and colonization, Latin spread across the Italian peninsula and eventually became the dominant language of the Roman Empire, forming the foundation for the Romance languages.
